A sofa's frame is usually made of wood, but can also be made of steel, plastic, or laminated boards. A quality sofa has a sturdy frame with glued and dowelled joints, and screwed corner blocks in important areas.

An age-old favourite, leather is a no-brainer when it comes to sofas of any style. It’s easy to clean, holds up well over time and comes in a variety of colours, finishes and textures that deliver on visual appeal. Leather sofa material is so popular because it has a stain-resistant finish, making it ideal for houses with high footfall in the living area. That said, leather is quite vulnerable to scratches and may develop cracks if the levels of humidity fluctuate.

The type of sofa you choose and how you arrange it depends on the room's layout, your comfort needs, and your personal preference. For example, an L-shaped sofa can be placed against a wall to maximize seating and floor space. A U-shaped sectional can be used for extra seating, while a cuddle sectional is good for snuggling.

The sofa you choose should fit your lifestyle and needs. Measure and create a floor plan of your room so you know the sofa will fit perfectly. Choose a sofa that’s both stylish and comfortable.
